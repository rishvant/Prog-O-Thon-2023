College Transportation Facility Guidelines

1. Conduct and Behavior:

Passengers are expected to behave in a respectful and orderly manner while using college transportation services.

Disruptive behavior, harassment, or any actions that create an unsafe environment will not be tolerated.

2. Smoking and Alcohol:

Smoking, vaping, and the consumption of alcohol are strictly prohibited on college transportation vehicles.

3. Vandalism and Damage:

Any form of vandalism or damage to transportation facilities, vehicles, or equipment will result in disciplinary action.

4. Non-Compliance with Instructions:

Passengers must follow the instructions and directives of transportation staff and drivers. Failure to comply with driver instructions, route changes, or emergency procedures may result in disciplinary action.

5. Fare Evasion:

Traveling without Institute I-card or appropriate payment may result in fines or other penalties.

6. Vandalism and Damage:

Any form of vandalism or damage to transportation facilities, vehicles, or equipment will result in disciplinary action.

7. Noise and Disturbances:

Passengers are expected to keep noise levels to a minimum to avoid disturbing others.

8. Personal Belongings:

Passengers are responsible for their personal belongings and must not obstruct aisles or exits with luggage or items.

9. Seat Belts:

Passengers must wear seat belts (if available) at all times while seated.

10. Safety Violations:

Tampering with safety equipment, emergency exits, or other safety features on transportation vehicles is strictly prohibited.

Disciplinary Actions

Clearly state the range of disciplinary actions that may be taken for violations, such as:

  • Verbal warnings
  • Written warnings
  • Suspension of transportation privileges
  • Fines or restitution for damages
  • Referral to campus authorities for further action
  • Legal consequences for severe violations
